About Me

A counselor grounded in hope

I am a Licensed Clinical Mental Health Counselor Associate (LCMHCA) Candidate based in North Carolina, completing my graduate training with a focus on serving adolescents and adults across the lifespan. My clinical interests include anxiety and depression, chronic illness and aging, grief and loss, addiction, and relationship concerns.

At the heart of my approach is a deep and abiding belief that change is possible — regardless of where someone is starting from. I view therapy as a collaborative, empowering process in which clients are the experts on their own lives. My practice is grounded in person-centered and strength-based principles, honoring each client's inherent resilience, wisdom, and capacity for growth. My role is to walk alongside them, offering a space that is safe, nonjudgmental, and oriented toward healing.

I draw on an integrative toolkit of evidence-based approaches — including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), Narrative Therapy, and Motivational Interviewing (MI) — tailoring techniques to meet each client where they are and in alignment with their unique goals, values, and lived experience.

I am committed to culturally responsive, evidence-informed practice and to building therapeutic relationships rooted in trust, warmth, and genuine care.

Professional Development

A roadmap for growth

My professional goals reflect a commitment to intentional, values-driven development as I move from graduate training into clinical practice and toward independent licensure.

At Graduation

Obtain Associate Licensure & Enter Group Practice

After receiving my degree, my goal is to practice clinically at an established group practice. To do so, I will finalize my application for associate licensure with the NC Board of Licensed Clinical Mental Health Counselors and seek employment opportunities that allow me to work with diverse client populations and connect me with a network of experienced mental health clinicians from whom I can learn.

After One Year

Build Clinical Competency Through Focused Growth

My goal for my first year of practice is to increase my clinical competency by identifying and pursuing continuing education in focused areas of professional interest. Potential areas I am considering include Motivational Interviewing, EMDR, and Dialectical Behavior Therapy — though I want to learn from my first year of clinical practice to determine what skills would be most relevant to my work moving forward.

After Three Years

Solidify Professional Identity as a Licensed Clinician

By year three, my goal is to begin solidifying my professional identity as a fully licensed clinical mental health counselor. Although I see my future self as a generalist, I would like to develop expertise in a few specialized areas — including older adults and those with disabilities. If this interest persists, I will pursue Medicare credentialing upon obtaining my LCMHC to serve these populations. My first three years of general practice will guide where I ultimately choose to focus.
