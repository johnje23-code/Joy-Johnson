Artifacts Pertinent to Professional Practice

The following four areas represent critical competencies in professional mental health practice. Each section highlights artifacts in domains central to safe, ethical, and effective clinical service.

Suicide Assessment & Intervention

Risk Assessment & Safety Planning

Suicide and Serious Mental Illness: An Overview of Considerations, Assessment, and Safety Planning

This artifact is an excellent resource that summarizes considerations relevant to suicide risk assessment and safety planning. Responding to clients with potential suicidal ideation according to recommended best practices is essential to promote client safety. This is a critical skill I need to practice ethically and legally in the group practice I hope to join.

Ethical Decision-Making

Ethics

Practitioner's Guide to Ethical Decision Making & The ACA Code of Ethics

These artifacts are essential references to ensure I am practicing within ethical mandates. The Practitioner's Guide provides a framework for structuring the decision-making process, which is important for protecting both clients and me as a clinical mental health practitioner. It is also vital to review the ACA Code of Ethics when facing ethical dilemmas.

Trauma-Informed Clinical Practice

Training

Foundations of Trauma-Informed Therapy Practices

This artifact is a highly informative resource for foundational knowledge about the causes and consequences of trauma, trauma-related disorders, and trauma-informed approaches to clinical care. The widespread prevalence of trauma necessitates that I have an understanding of how trauma may impact clients and how to respond through a trauma-informed lens.

Positive Psychotherapy Interventions

Strength-Based

This artifact includes demonstrations of eight positive psychotherapy techniques to promote client well-being.
